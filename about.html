From Accra to the Bar

Karen Safo-Barnieh is a Ghanaian-born, UK-based barrister, speaker and changemaker. At 17, she founded Youth Development Ghana, a grassroots initiative aimed at emancipating communities from the injustice of unfair trade. That instinct to use systems for justice has shaped everything since.

With a background spanning international development, natural resource governance and frontline legal practice, Karen brings a unique perspective to every case, every platform and every room she enters. She has been recognised as one of Britain's Future Leaders and named in the Future of Ghana 30 Under 30.

Legal practice

Karen Safo-Barnieh specialises in employment disputes, housing disrepair and contract issues. She is a member of Francis Taylor Building, one of the UK's leading planning, environment and public law chambers. Her approach combines strategic precision with genuine care for the people she represents.

Upheld

Karen is the founder of Upheld, a legal technology platform that puts employment, housing and contract rights directly in the hands of those who need them. Upheld guides users from initial assessment through to resolution, making legal knowledge accessible to everyone.

Speaking and advisory

Karen Safo-Barnieh speaks and advises on AI governance, natural resource governance, climate justice and international development. She has contributed to the BBC, The Independent, UN Women and the Congressional Black Caucus, and brings a perspective rooted in both the courtroom and the communities most affected by global policy.
