About

A life dedicated
to justice.

I'm Karen Safo-Barnieh — a Ghanaian-born, UK-based Barrister at Law and human rights activist. At 17, I founded Youth Development Ghana, a project aimed at emancipating communities from the injustice of unfair trade.

With a background spanning international development, natural resource governance, and frontline legal practice, I bring a unique perspective to every case. Recognised as one of Britain's Future Leaders, a Future of Ghana 30 Under 30 honouree, and a recipient of the Dean's Fellowship Award and a scholarship from UCLA.

Advisory

Beyond the courtroom.

Drawing on a decade of work in international development, natural resource governance, and human rights, I advise organisations, governments, and institutions on the forces reshaping the Global South — from AI and extractive industries to climate policy and environmental justice.

01

AI Governance

Advisory on AI regulation, ethics, and deployment in developing economies. Helping governments and organisations navigate the policy landscape of artificial intelligence — ensuring the Global South shapes the rules, not just follows them.

02
