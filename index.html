About

Social housing
to the Bar,
the UN, and beyond.

Born in Ghana and raised by a single mother in social housing in the UK, I self-funded my way through law school, working multiple jobs to reach the Bar. At 17 I founded my first organisation, Youth Development Ghana, campaigning against unfair trade and child labour. I have not stopped since.

I have spoken at Bloomberg, the Congressional Black Caucus, the UN, and the Independent Office for Police Conduct. I have served as a board member and legal adviser to organisations including the Association for Family Therapy and Systemic Practice and Sport England. I am the founder of Upheld, a legal technology platform putting rights directly in people's hands.
