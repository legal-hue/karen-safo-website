About Me

Using the law as a
tool for change.

I'm Karen Safo-Barnieh — a Ghanaian-born, UK-based Barrister at Law and human rights activist. At the age of 17, I founded Youth Development Ghana, a project aiming to emancipate communities from the injustice derived from unfair trade. That drive to use the law as a force for good has defined my career ever since.

With a background spanning the international development sector, natural resource governance, and frontline legal practice, I bring a unique perspective to every case. I've been recognised as one of Britain's Future Leaders, a Future of Ghana 30 Under 30 honouree, and a recipient of the Dean's Fellowship Award and a scholarship from UCLA.

Today, I specialise in employment disputes, housing disrepair, and contract law — working to make the legal process transparent, accessible, and empowering for every client.
